The problem

Delhi's air is a moving, time-critical target

PM2.5 hotspots flare unpredictably with traffic surges, industrial haze, inversions and road & construction dust resuspended by passing traffic. Static smog towers cannot follow the pollution — mitigation has to be mobile, predictive and coordinated.

The solution

One brain, many mobile actuators

Central AI Coordinator

Global telemetry of the network and fleet. Predicts spikes, dispatches sub-fleets, handles exceptions, enforces the supply lifecycle.

Mobile tanker fleet

Heterogeneous water-sprinkler trucks (3000 / 5000 / 12000 L) with IoT misting cannons. Drivers steer; the AI dictates the route.

Delhi Metro network

~50 real stations across all lines plus 6 water/charging depots form the directed road graph the fleet operates on.

Road cleaning & dust suppression

Beyond misting the air, the tankers wet-wash the road surface — binding settled road and construction dust so it stays on the ground instead of being kicked back up as PM10 / PM2.5 by passing traffic.

Why "agentic"

Perceive (live AQI + traffic), reason (predict, score, route), act (dispatch, spray, replenish) in a continuous closed loop — with no human in the routing decision.

Domain model

A directed graph with live, stateful telemetry

  • Nodes (V): metro stations carrying dynamic PM2.5 and traffic influx, with rolling history for trend analytics.
  • Special nodes: Sewage Treatment Plants (recycled water) and CNG/EV depots (energy) — modelled as service subclasses via an abstract base.
  • Edges (E): stateful road segments whose congestion factor and emission penalty mutate every tick.
  • Geometry: every edge snapped to real road shapes (OSRM) so paths follow actual streets.

Mathematical core

Modified multi-objective Dijkstra

Edge weight is not static distance — it blends congestion-stretched travel time with a dynamic, vehicle-aware emission overhead.

# cost of crossing edge e with a given vehicle
W(e) = ( distance_km / base_speed ) × traffic_density_factor + emission_penalty

# levied only when a heavy chassis idles into an already-critical cell
emission_penalty = COEF × emission_weight(vehicle) × traffic_factor × severity

Vehicle-aware

Mini / Medium / Heavy get different optimal paths over the same graph at the same instant.

Dynamic

Weights recomputed from live telemetry each query; gridlock reroutes onto open bypasses.

Efficient

Binary-heap single-source Dijkstra, O((V+E) log V), run in Python and in the browser.

Agentic workflows

Four autonomous behaviours, every tick

1

Predictive spike pre-emption

A >40% influx rise over two ticks flags an imminent breach and sends a pre-emptive curtain before the sensor confirms it.

2

Workload split + ETA sync

An efficiency-score matrix picks a 2-3 truck sub-fleet and staggers departures so each arrives as the previous empties.

3

Stuck-in-traffic exceptions

Below 5 km/h: mist in place if inside the zone, or revoke the route and re-allocate the load to an unblocked unit.

4

Closed-loop supply lifecycle

Below 15% water / 20% fuel, the AI locks the console, routes to the nearest depot, then reintegrates at 95%.

EcoShield Delhi07
Exception logic in depth

The two "stuck in traffic" branches

Inside the hotspot zone

Stationary misting curtain

If a truck is trapped within the target zone, it engages its cannons in place — a pitch-adjusted curtain knocking down the idling exhaust of the surrounding gridlock. The mission is kept.

Far from the hotspot

En-route interception

If trapped mid-transit, the AI revokes the target, locks the console, marks the unit STUCK, recomputes the graph, and re-allocates the workload to a moving unit with an open bypass.

Recovery: stuck units rejoin the reserve automatically once their segment clears; the displaced workload is topped up by the next dispatch pass.

Role-based access

The human steers, the AI dictates

  • A restricted in-cab Driver Console shows the assigned mission, the Dijkstra route, and water/charge gauges.
  • Admin modules are locked for drivers — bounded actuator only.
  • The admin can open any driver's console (one-way hierarchy).
  • A "console locked" banner appears whenever the AI overrides the route.
